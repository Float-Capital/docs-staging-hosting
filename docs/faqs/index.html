The basics

How do I get started in Float Capital?#

It is really simple to get started:

  1. Connect your wallet to the app.
  2. Make sure you have some DAI to stake a position. Check out this beginner‚Äôs guide on how to inject funds into your wallet: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XqTh5sQB8hI&t=2s&ab_channel=Float.Capital
  3. To make and pay for transactions you will need to obtain some MATIC from https://matic.supply/.
  4. Head over to the ‚Äúmarkets‚Äù channel to mint a long or short position in the protocol.
  5. Stake your long or short tokens to earn FLT tokens (alphaFLT tokens), Floats governance tokens.
  6. If you're really lost and want a hands-on guide, check out our beginner‚Äôs guide to Float Capital series: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7KUJGY1Zxyo&list=PL7RT-0ybd7jqbTHvG0O4rQfeR3yruypCf

What is a magic internet asset?#

A magic internet asset simulates the performance of an underlying asset but with some key benefits. Magic Internet Assets can offer investors reduced trading costs, tailored cash flow patterns, altered risk profiles, leverage and so on. Float Capital, however, is designed to be simple and safe.

Buying a magic internet asset allows you to get identical exposure to the risk and return benefits of the asset, without having to own the underlying asset.

What are ‚Äòlong‚Äô and 'short' tokens?#

For every market we offer (eg. The Flipp3ning), users can create long or short tokens for this market.

Holding long tokens allows you to benefit from the price appreciation üìà of the underlying market. Holding short tokens allows you to benefit from the price depreciation üìâ of the underlying market.

What is an oracle?#

Simply put, it's a feed that communicates information from the real world to the blockchain. For the purposes of trading magic internet assets, the oracle would provide the price feed for the underlying asset.

Float Capital has integrated Chainlink‚Äôs incredible price feeds into the protocol to provide the most accurate and efficient data for our magic internet assets.

What is a governance token?#

Governance tokens give holders voting rights over proposed revisions to the protocol, allowing them to have their voices heard on how a protocol operates. Some governance tokens (Float tokens included) give holders added benefits.

Similar to token buybacks, the fees earned by the protocol that are not paid out to holders in return for helping to rebalance the liquidity pools, will be used to buy back FLT tokens on the open market, thus decreasing the supply in circulation, and increasing their value.

Some common protocol questions

Why call it Float Capital?#

Exposure to magic internet assets ‚Äòfloat‚Äô in a band between 0% to 100%. The protocol uses various strong¬†incentives¬†to ensure that magic internet asset exposure is near 100%.

Are there any trading fees?#

Float Capital currently doesn't take any trading fees from traders. Fees are only levied when users¬†unstake¬†their position at a fee of 50 basis points. All fees levied accrue towards the treasury used to provide price support for FLT token.

What are the benefits of adding good liquidity?#

What is Good liquidity? It is liquidity added to the side that will rebalance the market (i.e. bringing Long (L) and Short (S) sides closer together) For example, if L > S, then additional Short liquidity (to the extent that it does not exceed Long) will be considered good liquidity.

Good liquidity¬†earns a higher percentage of fees from the protocol.¬†Good liquidity¬†also earns a supercharged yield, through earning yield on all the underlying assets. Finally, staking good liquidity also allows users to earn FLT tokens at a higher rate. The¬†whitepaper¬†provides comprehensive details on these incentives.

Can I get liquidated?#

Absolutely not.

The platform has been designed in such a way that it doesn‚Äôt require liquidations or collateralised debt positions.

Our markets are peer-to-peer, meaning that capital moves between long and short positions in each market based on the underlying price movement.

To remove the need for liquidations, your exposure ‚Äòfloats‚Äô based on the balance of capital in the market. If you‚Äôre on the under-balanced side (less capital) you will always have 100% exposure. If you‚Äôre on the over-balanced side (more capital) your your exposure will float according to the balance between the over-balanced and under-balanced sides of the market.

Exposure

What is exposure and how does it work?#

Exposure represents how closely your position tracks the price of the underlying asset.

In Float Capital your exposure ‚Äòfloats‚Äô. That means that based on the balance of the market you‚Äôre in, your exposure to price movements can change. It‚Äôs an awesome innovation that means we don‚Äôt have to require over-collateralized debt positions or liquidations.

The leverage of a market interacts with the exposure on the long and short sides of that market. To get your ‚Äòcurrent exposure‚Äô you simply need to multiply the market‚Äôs leverage by the exposure shown on the market page.

For example:

If the price of the underlying asset moves 10%, and the exposure is 100%, then the asset will move 10%. If the exposure was 80%, then the asset would move 8% (80% 10%) - this being true for both up and downward movements.*

Say you‚Äôre in a market with 100% exposure, and the market is 3x leveraged. If the price of the underlying asset moves 10% then the asset would move 30%.

Mathematically, if the percentage by which the investment changes, pIp_IpI‚Äã, is equal to the percentage by which the value of the underlying asset's property changes, pap_apa‚Äã, then the exposure is said to be 100%. The exposure can be seen as the factor that pIp_IpI‚Äã must be multiplied by in order to get pap_apa‚Äã. This can be formalized as follows:

Are my positions locked into the exposure percentage at the time of minting?#

No, they are not locked in at the time of minting. The percentage will dynamically fluctuate for all users. This trade-off is needed for the system to scale rapidly and effectively.

We allow arbitrary entry and exit of liquidity without price slippage. This result is fluctuating exposure.

How frequently does the exposure change, and what is the maximum percentage it can change in a day?#

The exposure is a function of liquidity. The under-balanced side/over-balanced side gives the exposure, so the exposure can change depending on the liquidity put into both sides of the market.

Float Capital uses a novel mechanism called ‚ÄòNext Price Execution‚Äô which is used to prevent and protect against front-running attacks.

This means that all the users‚Äô actions will be executed once the price updates, according to the heartbeat of that price feed - this is also when the liquidity between longs and shorts will shift due to price change.

As markets scale and fill with more capital, big shifts in exposure will become less and less common.

For trading strategies that rely on having a certain level of exposure, we recommend checking the exposure of your position.

Incentives

What is the Float multiplier, and how does it work?#

This is the percentage at which a user farms/earns FLT (currently alphaFLT) when providing liquidity to the under-balanced side of the market.

In order for a user to earn/farm these rewards they would have to stake their magic internet asset in the protocol. Users who provide liquidity in the under-balanced side of the market earn a larger amount of FLT tokens.

Example:

Say the Float multiplier on the under-balanced side of the market was sitting at 150%.

This would mean users providing liquidity to the under-balanced side of the market would earn/farm 50% more FLT for rebalancing the market than if they were to enter the over-balanced side of the market.

What is a funding rate?#

A funding rate is small payment made from the over-balanced side to the under-balanced side, to incentivize more balance in the market. As the markets become more imbalanced, the funding will rate increase.

We set a maximum funding rate for the over-balanced side that would only be achieved if the market were completely imbalanced (i.e. there was no liquidity on the under-balanced side). We then scale the funding rate linearly based on the imbalance.

Read this blog article for more info.

This is the exact solidity code used to calculate funding rate:

    fundingAmount =      ((overbalancedValue - underbalancedValue) *        _fundingRateMultiplier_e18 *        (block.timestamp - lastUpdateTimestamp)) /      SECONDS_IN_A_YEAR_e18;

And this code is run on every system update, so it is a lot of little funding rate amounts moved from overbalanced to underbalanced. Let's use the example where

  • overbalanced side is 75% of total liquidity
  • underbalanced side is 25% of total liquidity we get the following (ignoring the e18 complication)
fundingAmount = (0.75 * totalLiquidity - 0.25 * totalLiquidity) * fundingRateMultiplier * deltaTimeInSeconds / secondsInAYear// impliesfundingAmount = 0.5 * totalLiquidity * fundingRateMultiplier * deltaTimeInSeconds / secondsInAYear

So, supposing that the market stays at 75/25 for a year then all the little funding rate bits are the same so we can lump them together:

// system update at time t_1chunk_t_1 = 0.5 * totalLiquidity * fundingRateMultiplier * dt_1_inSeconds / secondsInAYearoverbalanced_side -= chunk_t_1underbalanced_side += chunk_t_1...// more system updates.....// assume price change or new liquidity occurs such that we still have 25/75 split...// system update at time t_nchunk_t_n = 0.5 * totalLiquidity * fundingRateMultiplier * dt_n_inSeconds / secondsInAYearoverbalanced_side -= chunk_t_nunderbalanced_side += chunk_t_n

so the total amount of funding rate given to the underbalanced side is

chunk_t_1 + ... + chunk_t_n = fundingAmount = 0.5 * totalLiquidity * fundingRateMultiplier * (dt_1_inSeconds + ... + dt_n_inSeconds) / secondsInAYear

So if the time period is over a year then (dt_1_inSeconds + ... + dt_n_inSeconds) = oneYearInSeconds which implies that the total amount given to the underbalanced side is

fundingAmount = 0.5 * totalLiquidity * fundingRateMultiplier

What is the APY composed of?#

The APY is composed of:

1. Yield enhancement

All the DAI used to mint positions in the pools are sent to a yield provider (Aave, BENQI and Trader Joe). As the market becomes imbalanced, the yield is increasingly allocated to the under-balanced sides of the market. A dynamic portion of the yield is also sent to the treasury for providing FLT token price support.

2. Funding rates

Our funding rate scales linearly with imbalance to incentivise positions on the under-balanced side. This varies in each market. On OHM v2 it scales up to a maximum of 800% APY.

How does the FLT issuance work?#

FLT token (currently alphaFLT) issuance is calculated by the size of your position, duration staked, and the market imbalance.

Note: That since the aFLT token is not tradable, it does not have a price, and it won‚Äôt get factored into the APY.

Is alphaFLT transferable? / Can I trade alphaFLT?#

AlphaFLT is non-transferrable and cannot be traded.

We‚Äôve made this decision for two reasons:

  1. We want to build a community around an awesome product, not around pumping a token price.
  2. We want to refine the tokenomics behind FLT as best as possible so that we can launch a token that becomes a DeFi blue chip.

When we‚Äôre happy with this, we‚Äôll replace alphaFLT with FLT, and alphaFLT holders will be able to redeem their tokens for FLT.

Trading

How often are price updates / Do I have to manually update the price after a transaction?#

Users don't have to manually update the price.

Our price feeds update in line with each ‚Äòheartbeat‚Äô in the Chainlink price feed.

For most markets, this can take between 5-30mins.

Suggestion: The system requires a transaction to get the new price from Chainlink. The function that needs to be called is public and anyone can call it. It is also a requirement for most market actions (mint/redeem/stake) so in a busy market the heartbeat will always match the oracle‚Äôs. To make sure that even non-busy markets get frequent updates we run a bot that calls the update function on a regular interval (~5min).

You can find more about the heartbeats of various price feeds in the links below:

  • Polygon: https://docs.chain.link/docs/matic-addresses/
  • Avalanche: https://docs.chain.link/docs/avalanche-price-feeds/

How does the protocol ensure there is enough DAI for redemptions?#

Our floating exposure ensures that there is always sufficient collateral for redemptions.

For example: If long liquidity is 10% and short liquidity is 90%, the exposure for the short side will be 10% / 90% = 11.11% and exposure for long will be 100%. Therefore the long side will have 100% exposure to 50% price drop whereas the short side will only have 11.11% exposure - this ensures that the ratio of total DAI to sum of long and short liquidity is always 1:1.

What is volatility decay?#

Volatility decay is a phenomenon observed when investing in leveraged assets. The decay in a traders‚Äô profit and loss is due to frequent directional changes in the price of the underlying asset.

Learn more about it in our blog.
